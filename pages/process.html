How to evaluate the samples

What the samples are

Sanitized, realistic examples intended to show structure, clarity, and decision points.

What the samples are not

Not copies of internal tooling, system diagrams, security controls, or real identifiers.

What to look for

Scanability, step quality, escalation logic, verification criteria, and error-proofing.

Publishing rules safe
  • No proprietary names, customer identifiers, site identifiers, internal URLs, or screenshots.
  • Keep steps realistic but generic (the “shape” of the work, not the sensitive specifics).
  • Include verification and closeout steps when appropriate.
  • Track updates in a “Changes” block per document and in the site changelog.
